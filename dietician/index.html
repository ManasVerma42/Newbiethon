Vegan Diet

A vegan diet excludes all animal products, including meat, dairy, eggs, and honey. It is rich in fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, legumes, and whole grains, providing essential nutrients like fiber, vitamins, and minerals. Veganism is also often chosen for ethical, environmental, and health reasons.

High Protein Diet

A high protein diet typically includes foods like meat, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, and nuts, prioritizing protein-rich sources. This diet is often favored by athletes and individuals aiming for muscle gain or weight loss, as protein helps with muscle repair and satiety. However, it's important to balance protein intake with other nutrients for overall health.

Keto Diet

The ketogenic (keto) diet is a high-fat, low-carbohydrate eating plan that aims to induce ketosis, a metabolic state where the body uses fats for energy instead of carbohydrates. This diet emphasizes foods like meat, fish, eggs, nuts, seeds, oils, and non-starchy vegetables while limiting carbohydrates from grains, sugars, fruits, and some vegetables. It's popular for weight loss and may offer benefits for certain medical conditions, but it requires careful planning to ensure nutrient adequacy.

Low Carb Diet

A low-carb diet restricts carbohydrate intake, typically focusing on protein, fats, and non-starchy vegetables. This approach can help stabilize blood sugar levels and promote weight loss by encouraging the body to burn stored fat for energy. Common low-carb diets include the ketogenic diet and Atkins diet.
